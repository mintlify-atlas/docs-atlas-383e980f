Open Brain is the infrastructure layer for your thinking. One Supabase database with vector search, one MCP server deployed as an Edge Function, and any AI you use can read and write to the same persistent knowledge base — Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, Claude Code, whatever ships next month.

This isn't a notes app. It's an open protocol built so every AI tool shares the same memory of you.

## What you're building

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your Supabase project">
    A PostgreSQL database with pgvector enabled — your persistent memory store. Free tier. Takes 2 minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deploy the MCP server">
    One Supabase Edge Function that gives any AI four tools: capture, search, list, and stats. No local servers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your AI clients">
    Claude Desktop, ChatGPT, Claude Code, Cursor — paste one URL and you're connected. Any MCP client works.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start building extensions">
    The core system is your foundation. Extensions, recipes, and skills build on top of it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What your AI can do

Once connected, your AI can naturally capture and retrieve anything you've shared with it:

| You say... | Tool used |
|---|---|
| "Remember this: decided to push the launch to Q2" | `capture_thought` |
| "What did I capture about career changes?" | `search_thoughts` |
| "Show me what I captured this week" | `list_thoughts` |
| "How many thoughts do I have by topic?" | `thought_stats` |

<Note>
  Open Brain works across every AI client at once. A thought captured in Claude Desktop is immediately searchable from ChatGPT, Cursor, or Claude Code.
</Note>
